Cloud Outages vs On-Prem: What’s Really Happening?

Cloud outages are more visible lately — major providers sometimes fail and the blast radius is large. Click to read.

Posted: Dec 2025

Cloud outages have become more noticeable lately. Big providers like AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure occasionally experience service disruptions, and when they do, the impact spreads quickly because so many organizations depend on them. Even though cloud platforms offer great scalability and convenience, these incidents show that no environment is immune to failure.

On the other side, businesses that run their services on-premise avoid widespread cloud outages, but they carry the responsibility of maintaining uptime themselves. That means investing in power redundancy, cooling, security, patching, monitoring, and disaster recovery. It’s more control, but also more work.

In the end, neither approach is perfect. Cloud gives you speed and flexibility, while on-prem gives you control and independence. The smart move for most companies today is a balanced hybrid model: keep critical workloads on-prem and leverage the cloud for scaling, backups, or non-critical services. This reduces the blast radius of outages and builds stronger resilience overall.
